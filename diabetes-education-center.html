Type 1 Diabetes (T1D)

Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune disease where the body's immune system destroys the insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas. Because of this, the body cannot produce insulin, so people must take insulin for life.

infoInsulin helps move glucose (sugar) from the blood into cells for energy.

gpp_badThe Cause

The immune system attacks and destroys insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas. It is not caused by diet or lifestyle.

vaccinesThe Treatment

Lifelong insulin therapy is required. Managed through multiple daily injections or an insulin pump.

2

What is Type 2 Diabetes?

Insulin Resistance

In Type 2 diabetes, the body's cells become resistant to insulin. The pancreas tries to compensate by producing more insulin, but eventually can't keep up, leading to elevated blood sugar.

Risk Factors

  • circleBeing overweight or obese
  • circleFamily history of diabetes
  • circlePhysical inactivity
  • circleAge over 45

What is Gestational Diabetes?

Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM) is high blood sugar that develops during pregnancy in a woman who did not previously have diabetes. It usually appears in the 2nd or 3rd trimester because pregnancy hormones cause insulin resistance, making it harder for the body to control glucose levels.

info

After delivery, blood sugar often returns to normal — but the mother has a higher risk of developing Type 2 Diabetes later in life.

Screening for GDM

schedule
